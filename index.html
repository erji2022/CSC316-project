Climate Change Trends

Global warming is one of the most pressing challenges of our time, driven largely by human-induced changes to the Earth’s atmosphere. This visualization presents long-term trends in global temperature, atmospheric CO₂ levels, and sea level rise, offering a clear view of how these indicators have evolved together over recent decades.

Air Quality Degradation: Polluted Skies

Air pollution poses a severe risk to public health. PM2.5, in particular, can cause serious respiratory diseases and increase the mortality rate. Despite some decreases in overall pollution levels, PM2.5 exposure remains many times higher than what the WHO recommends.

Explore the Tree Map to compare the averages between regions and the trends of each country. See how each region and country are contributing to air pollution of the globe.
